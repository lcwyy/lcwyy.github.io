Personal statement
I recently obtained my doctorate in civil engineering at Tongji University, P. R. China. My research in the broader field of geotechnical engineering focuses on numerical simulation and computational geomechanics.  I have great interest in finite element (FE) method, discrete element (DE) method, and computational multiscale approaches (e.g. coupled DE-FE mthod). My Ph.D. dissertation title is "Disturbance Mechanism and Application of the Whole Shield-driving Process based on Multi-scale Approach", and is funded by National Basic Research Program of China (973 Program) and National Natural Science Foundation of China.
During my doctoral research, I spent most of my effort on numerical simulation of tunnel excavation. The computational methods I used in the research include FEM (finite element method), DEM (discrete element method), coupled DE-FE method, and CFD (computational fluid dynamics). I always endeavour to bridge the sophisticated computational approaches and the geotechnical practice. Thus, my work can be mainly divided into two topics:
  • Geotechnical engineering practice, e.g. field monitoring, in-situ tests, and laboratory tests.
  • Numerical simulation of multiple problems encountered in practice, e.g. ground and structure responses to tunneling, tunnel face stability, and coupled fluid-solid problems during excavation.
